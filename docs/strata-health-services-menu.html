Section Ten · Questions
Frequently Asked
Section Ten

Things people ask.

The questions that come up on every first call. If yours isn't here, send it — we'd rather answer it once for you than dance around it on a sales pitch.

Are you HIPAA compliant?
We sign a BAA, and HIPAA lives in the build itself — encryption, audit logs, access controls, break-glass policies, training. We also do the boring documentation auditors actually ask for: risk assessments, sanction policy, breach SOP, training logs.
Do you support my EHR?
Probably. We've integrated with Epic, Athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, DrChrono, AdvancedMD, NextGen, Valant, Practice Fusion, and others. If yours isn't on the list, we'll quote it after a 30-minute discovery call.
Who owns the AI models you build for me?
You do. Custom-trained models are deployed in your tenant, weights stored in your storage, training data kept under your BAA. You can take them anywhere — we just write good ones.
Can you migrate my data from another vendor?
Yes. PHI-safe transfer with chain-of-custody documentation, secure-wipe certificates for retired drives, and a full audit trail. Migrations are quoted per scope after we've inventoried the source system.
What's a realistic timeline?
Solo practice: 6 weeks. Multi-provider clinic: 10 – 12 weeks. Specialty group: 16 – 20 weeks. Most delays come from EHR vendor BAAs, which we kick off on day one to keep them off the critical path.
Do you handle OCR responses or breach filings?
No. We do the technical and operational HIPAA work — risk assessments, safeguards, audits, remediation, audit-ready documentation. For OCR responses or breach-notification filings, we coordinate with your healthcare counsel.
Do you take equity in lieu of fees?
Sometimes. For early-stage healthtech founders we'll consider a hybrid arrangement. For established practices: cash, terms, and milestones — that's how everyone stays aligned.
What if my staff hates the new system?
We train them, then we hyper-care for 30 days. If a workflow doesn't fit how the front desk actually works, we change it. Adoption is part of the deliverable, not your problem to solve alone.
Where are you based?
Oxford, Mississippi. We work remotely across the US, on-site for launches and as needed for clinic infrastructure work. Travel billed at cost when applicable.

The Strata Story

Things we believe.

What we believe

Healthcare software should be built like a building. Permits before drywall, foundations before paint, a paper trail at every step.
Compliance isn't a checkbox at the end. It's the slab. We pour it first.
AI in clinical settings should be owned by the clinic — not rented from a chatbot company that read about medicine on the internet.
The front desk has more in common with a job-site superintendent than people realize. Both run the day. We design for them.
"It works on my machine" is not a deliverable. We hand off systems with documentation an auditor can read.
